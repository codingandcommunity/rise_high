What is cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is a type of bullying that takes place over the internet, including through social media apps, texting, and online gaming.

Before we learn more about cyberbullying, let's learn some of the basics about bullying...

What is bullying?

According to stopbullying.gov, bullying is defined as "Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior among school aged children that involves a real or perceived power imbalance." To be bullying these criteria must be met:

  • Repeated action or threats
  • A power imbalance
  • Intention to cause harm

Here are some of the main types of bullying:

  • Verbal: Teasing, name-calling, and threats of violence

  • Physical: Physically attacking someone or stealing someone else's posessions
  • Cyberbulling: Sending, posting, sharing negative, harmful, false, mean content about someone else
